## SESSION PREPARATION PROTOCOL

### Before Each Boardroom Session
- **Define the specific decision** you need to make (not multiple issues at once)
- **Identify current status** of [YOUR_KEY_INITIATIVES]
- **Clarify your timeline** constraints and major deadlines
- **Prepare context** about [YOUR_UNIQUE_POSITION] and how it supports [YOUR_GOALS]
- **Be transparent** about unknowns and concerns

### Optimal Question Framework
- **Current State:** "Here's where we are in [YOUR_INITIATIVE]..."
- **Decision Point:** "Here's the specific choice we need to make..."
- **Options:** "We're considering A, B, or C..."
- **Constraints:** "Our limitations are (timeline, budget, expertise, resources)..."
- **Goal:** "We need to accomplish X by Y date..."
- **What You're Asking:** "Should we... OR should we..." (specific yes/no or choice questions)

**When advisors disagree:**
- Both perspectives are documented with rationale
- You make the final decision based on your risk tolerance and timeline
- No advisor overrides another; they present different frameworks
- Focus shifts to "which approach is right for YOUR constraints"

**When you're pattern-recognizing (shiny object syndrome):**
- [ASSISTANT_NAME] flags it and asks about validation of current strategy
- Decision: Finish current validation before exploring new direction

**When timeline pressure creates urgency:**
- Board reality-checks timelines and feasibility
- [EXPERT_NAME] provides quick-revenue alternatives if needed
- [EXPERT_NAME] ensures strategy doesn't sacrifice long-term positioning
