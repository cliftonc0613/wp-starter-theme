## PHASE 1: TEACH (45 minutes)
### What is AI, Really?

**üéØ Goal:** Demystify AI so students feel capable (not overwhelmed)

---

### **0:00-0:10 | Welcome & Icebreaker (10 min)**

**ENERGY CHECK:** Start with energy. You set the tone.

**Script:**
"Welcome! I'm excited you're here. Before we jump in, let's get to know each other.

Poll: **Quick show of hands‚Äîwho's here because:**
- A. You're curious about AI?
- B. You want better job prospects?
- C. You heard AI is the future?
- D. All of the above?

**Good news:** All of these are valid reasons, and we're covering all of it today.

Poll: **Who here has used ChatGPT before?**
- If yes: Great! We're going deeper.
- If no: Perfect! You're about to have your mind blown.

Let me give you 60 seconds of truth: **You don't need to be a programmer to learn AI.** You don't need advanced math. You need curiosity and willingness to try. That's it."

**KEY MESSAGE:** Confidence + Accessibility. Repeat if needed.

---

### **0:10-0:25 | "What is AI?" - 3-Minute Versions (15 min)**

**Show a 2-minute video** (pre-recorded or find on YouTube):
- ChatGPT writing an email
- DALL-E generating an image
- Netflix recommending a show
- **Why:** Concrete examples beat abstract definitions

**After video:**

**Script:**
"You just saw three types of AI:
1. **Generative AI** (ChatGPT) ‚Äî creates new things
2. **Creative AI** (DALL-E) ‚Äî makes images
3. **Predictive AI** (Netflix) ‚Äî guesses what you want

But here's the KEY INSIGHT: They're all doing the SAME THING under the hood.

**What is AI, really?**

AI = **Machines learning patterns from data**

That's it. Not magic. Not some conspiracy. Patterns from data.

Example:
- Netflix saw **pattern:** People who watched Marvel watch more superhero content
- **Action:** Netflix recommends more superhero content to similar people
- **Result:** You think Netflix is psychic (it's not, it's patterns)

**Your Job in This Course:** Learn to work WITH these patterns, not against them."

**Draw on whiteboard (or screen share) simple diagram:**
```
Data ‚Üí Machine Learns Patterns ‚Üí Makes Predictions ‚Üí Takes Action
```

---

### **0:25-0:35 | Three Types of AI (10 min)**

**Script:**
"Let's be specific about three types of AI you'll see everywhere:

**Type 1: Predictive AI** (your job prospects: GOOD)
- What it does: Guesses what comes next
- Examples: Netflix recommendations, Amazon product suggestions, your email spam filter
- Real job: Predictive Maintenance Specialist ($60-75K)
  - *'We predict when factory equipment fails BEFORE it breaks'*

**Type 2: Generative AI** (hottest skill right now)
- What it does: Creates new content (text, images, code, videos)
- Examples: ChatGPT, DALL-E, Midjourney, Claude
- Real job: AI Content Strategist ($65-85K)
  - *'We use AI to create marketing content 10x faster'*

**Type 3: Generative AI for Analysis** (most valuable)
- What it does: Analyzes data and finds insights
- Examples: ChatGPT analyzing a dataset, Claude reviewing legal docs
- Real job: AI Business Analyst ($70-90K)
  - *'We use AI to find opportunities in data'*

**REALITY CHECK:** Most entry-level AI jobs combine all three. You'll learn all three."

---

### **0:35-0:45 | Career Stories (10 min)**

**Tell THREE specific stories (don't read, tell naturally):**

**Story 1: Marcus ‚Äî From Warehouse to Predictive Maintenance**
"Marcus worked in a warehouse. He got laid off when the company automated. He took this course. Learned AI fundamentals. Now? He works for a manufacturing company predicting when equipment fails. His salary: $68K starting. Why? Because he understood the PROBLEM (equipment failing = loss of money) and learned AI to SOLVE it."

*Pause. Let it land.*

**Story 2: Sarah ‚Äî From Sales to AI Sales Tools**
"Sarah was in sales for 10 years. Good at it, but burned out. She learned AI fundamentals in this course. Realized she could use AI to automate her lead qualifying. Now she runs an AI sales consulting practice helping other companies do the same. Her salary? $80K+ (and she's her own boss)."

**Story 3: Jamal ‚Äî From Customer Service to AI Ethics**
"Jamal worked customer service. Dealt with terrible chatbots every day. Frustrated. Took this course. Realized he wanted to build BETTER AI. Now he works for a consulting firm helping companies think through AI ethics. His salary: $72K. His path: This course + extra study + his deep understanding of the problem."

**Script (ending):**
"Here's why I'm telling you these stories: **None of them started as programmers.** None had data science degrees. They all had real-world experience, learned AI fundamentals, and became valuable because they could TRANSLATE between the technology and the real problem.

**That's exactly what we're building toward in this course.**"

### **0:55-2:45 | The Five Prompts (110 minutes)**

**STRUCTURE:** 5 prompts √ó 22 minutes each (20 min build + 2 min transition)

**Time Management:** Use a timer. Announce it. Keep moving.

---

#### **PROMPT 1: Customer Email Response (0:55-1:17)**

**Business Problem:**
"You work in customer support. A customer is frustrated. You need to write a response quickly. AI can help draft it."

**The Prompt:**
```
I work in customer support. A customer is frustrated because their order is 2 weeks late. 
Write a professional, empathetic response that:
- Apologizes sincerely
- Explains what we're doing to fix it
- Offers a solution (discount or replacement)

Customer complaint: [PASTE CUSTOMER EMAIL HERE]

Write the response now.
```

**What Students Do:**
1. Paste prompt into ChatGPT/Claude
2. Paste a sample angry customer email (you provide one, or they make one up)
3. Read AI's response
4. Copy BEST response to their handout
5. **Write 1-2 sentences:** "Why was this response good?"

**Instructor Circulate:**
- "How's it going?"
- "Do you see how the AI understood the EMOTION in the complaint?"
- "This is Empathy AI‚Äîvaluable skill"

**2-MIN TRANSITION:**
"Everyone save your best response. Hands up if you found this useful? Good. Move to Prompt 2."

---

#### **PROMPT 2: Meeting Summary (1:17-1:39)**

**Business Problem:**
"You attended a long meeting. You need notes fast. AI can summarize."

**The Prompt:**
```
I just attended a 45-minute meeting. Here's a transcript:

[PASTE MEETING TRANSCRIPT]

Summarize the meeting into:
- What was discussed (2 sentences)
- Action items (bulleted list)
- Deadlines (if mentioned)

Make it short. I have 5 minutes to read this.
```

**What Students Do:**
1. Use provided transcript (or find meeting transcript online, or make one up)
2. Paste into ChatGPT/Claude
3. Get summary back
4. Copy BEST summary to handout
5. **Write 1-2 sentences:** "How could this save you time at work?"

**Instructor Note:**
"Notice the AI pulled out action items automatically? This is Extractive AI. Corporations pay for this."

---

#### **PROMPT 3: Data Analysis (1:39-2:01)**

**Business Problem:**
"You have customer data. You want insights fast. AI can analyze."

**The Prompt:**
```
I have the following customer data:

Customer Age | Purchase Amount | Repeat Purchase?
18          | $45             | No
25          | $120            | Yes
32          | $85             | Yes
45          | $200            | Yes
19          | $30             | No
52          | $150            | Yes

What patterns do you see? Who should we target for marketing?
```

**What Students Do:**
1. Paste data into ChatGPT/Claude
2. Get AI analysis
3. Copy BEST analysis to handout
4. **Write 1-2 sentences:** "What business decision would you make based on this?"

**Instructor Note:**
"AI found the pattern: older customers spend more and return. That's a $10M insight for the right company."

---

#### **PROMPT 4: Content Creation (2:01-2:23)**

**Business Problem:**
"You need social media content. You want it fast and engaging. AI can help."

**The Prompt:**
```
Write 3 short social media posts for a coffee shop that:
- Are funny or engaging
- Mention our new seasonal drink (Pumpkin Spice Latte)
- Include a call-to-action (visit us, tag a friend, etc.)
- Are under 280 characters each

Make them feel authentic, not corporate.
```

**What Students Do:**
1. Paste prompt into ChatGPT/Claude
2. Get 3 social posts back
3. Copy BEST post to handout
4. **Write 1-2 sentences:** "Would you engage with this on social media?"

**Instructor Note:**
"Content creators use AI now. It doesn't replace their creativity‚Äîit speeds it up."

---

#### **PROMPT 5: Problem-Solving (2:23-2:45)**

**Business Problem:**
"You're stuck on a problem. You need ideas. AI can brainstorm."

**The Prompt:**
```
I work in retail. My problem: Customers come in, browse, but don't buy. 
They say the store is too crowded and overwhelming.

What are 5 specific things we could do to make the store feel less crowded?
Give practical, low-cost ideas we could implement this month.
```

**What Students Do:**
1. Paste prompt into ChatGPT/Claude
2. Get 5 ideas back
3. Copy BEST idea to handout
4. **Write 1-2 sentences:** "Which idea is most practical? Why?"

**Instructor Note:**
"This is Strategic AI‚Äîhelping humans think differently. Your job isn't to replace your brain; it's to upgrade it."

---

## PHASE 3: SHOWCASE (15 minutes)
### Student Presentations & Peer Validation

**üéØ Goal:** Celebrate work, build community, validate learning

---

### **2:45-3:00 | Showcase Protocol (15 min)**

**How It Works:**
"3-4 volunteers share their BEST prompt result. 2 minutes each. Everyone else gives feedback."

**Script:**
"Who wants to share first? No judgment. Just show us what you built."

**If Volunteers Hesitate:**
"Let me go first. Here's MY best prompt result [share example]. What do you notice?"

**For Each Volunteer:**

**Student Shares (1 min):**
- "Here's the business problem I solved..."
- "Here's the prompt I wrote..."
- "Here's what AI created..."

**You Ask (20 sec):**
"What made this work?"

**Peers Respond (20 sec):**
"That's clever because..." or "I'd use that for..."

**Key to Showcase:**
- Keep energy HIGH
- Celebrate every attempt
- Normalize "AI helps humans work better"
- Ask: "Who else could use this in your job?"

## DURING SESSION: TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Student can't access ChatGPT/Claude | Have backup laptop ready; use assistant's account |
| WiFi drops | Use mobile hotspot; continue on phone |
| Student says "I don't understand AI" | Tell them: "That's okay. Everyone starts here. Just follow the prompt." |
| Student finishes early | Ask: "Can you make your prompt better? What would a CEO ask?" |
| Student is frustrated | Remind: "You're building real skills. It's okay to struggle." |
| Group is way behind | Skip Prompt 5; they have 4. Better to complete than rush. |
| Student wants to dive deep technically | "Great question. Bookmark it. We'll go deep Week 2. Today, we build confidence." |
