## TROUBLESHOOTING THE TROUBLESHOOTING

**"What if my students already know ChatGPT?"**
‚Üí Great! They'll move faster. Ask them to make their prompts MORE sophisticated.

**"What if I'm running late on prompts?"**
‚Üí Skip Prompt 5 if you must. Better to complete 4 well than rush 5 badly.

**"What if internet dies?"**
‚Üí Show pre-recorded AI responses from your laptop. Students still see what's possible.

**"What if students say it's too easy?"**
‚Üí Challenge them: "Make your prompt so specific that ONLY your desired outcome works."

**"What if students say it's too hard?"**
‚Üí Remind them: "You're not supposed to be an expert. You're supposed to try. That's it."

## CONTINGENCY PLANS

**If attendance is low (60-70%):**
‚Üí Don't adjust pacing. Deliver the same session to the group that showed up.

**If attendance is high (25+ students):**
‚Üí Consider adding a TA or having students work in larger pods.
‚Üí Takes longer but maintains quality.

**If technical skill is lower than expected:**
‚Üí Spend extra time on TEACH phase (add 10 minutes)
‚Üí Simplify prompts by half (don't eliminate them)
‚Üí Offer more hands-on support during BUILD

**If technical skill is higher than expected:**
‚Üí Add an advanced challenge: "Make your prompt even better by asking a follow-up"
‚Üí Compress BUILD phase (finish early, extend Showcase)
‚Üí Use those students as peer mentors for others
